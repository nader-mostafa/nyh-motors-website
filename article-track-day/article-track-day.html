Track Day Essentials: What You Need to Know

April 5, 2026 5 min read By NYH Motors Driving Experience Team

A track day is every performance car owner's rite of passage. There is no substitute for the experience of pushing your car to its limits in a controlled, safe environment. If you've never attended one, this guide will prepare you for everything you need to know.

What Is a Track Day?

A track day is a non-competitive driving event held on a closed racing circuit. Unlike motorsport, there is no racing against other drivers — the goal is personal development, exploring your car's capabilities, and having an extraordinary experience in complete safety.

Pre-Event Car Preparation

Before your first track day, have your car inspected by a specialist. Key checks include:

Brake pads and discs — replace if less than 30% remaining
Tyre tread depth and tyre pressure — inflate to track-recommended pressures
Engine oil — full level, fresh change preferred
Coolant — full level, no leaks
Remove all loose items from the cabin — they become dangerous projectiles

What to Bring on the Day

  • A full-face or open-face crash helmet (most circuits hire these)
  • Comfortable, flat-soled driving shoes — no flip-flops or thick-soled boots
  • Lightweight, non-synthetic clothing — cotton or nomex preferred
  • Plenty of water and snacks — track driving is physically demanding
  • Gaffer tape to cover headlights and numberplates (protects from stone chips)

On-Track Etiquette

Track days have strict etiquette rules. Always point faster cars by — never block or race other participants. Observe yellow flag warnings and lift completely off the throttle. Never exceed the track's noise limit; many venues have strict sound restrictions.

After the Track

After a track day, your brakes, tyres and fluids will all be under more stress than usual. Schedule a post-track inspection at NYH Motors. We recommend a brake inspection, fluid top-up and tyre check after every track event to keep your car in peak condition.
